Obtaining Your Drivers License or Cal-ID

A birth certificate can be obtained in person in county born (usually should check each county) or by mail or on the Internet - must prove ID (should lookup for address of county recorder that providers birth certificates and confirm that birth certificates are for all years at that county recorder)

This process can be done online in some cases. For new Cal-ID or LL, people can get form and apply at DMV.

Find the Closest DMV Office to You: DMV Locator
  1. Must prove legal presence and ID (typically birth certificate or certificate naturalization or green card proves both)
    • A person trying to get a Cal-ID or DL without a birth certificate/certificate of naturalization/green card can present Legal Status Summary (with color photo) from CDCR, if ask parole agent or write to: CDCR Archives Unit 2015 Aerojet Rd., Suite D Rancho Cordova, 95742.
    • (In your letter, say you are requesting a Legal Status Summary to prove your birth date and legal presence to the DMV. You must include your name, CDCR number, phone number, signature, and a reliable address where the Archives Unit can mail you back the certification.)
    • NOTE: DMV should accept this document to prove legal presence and may accept it to prove identity (bring other things that might prove ID, as well)
  2. Social security card or at least number (exception for legally present non- citizen)
  3. 2 proofs of address for Real ID or 1 proof of address for regular ID (examples on DMV website are worth including in tech tool)
    • Can Get Real ID or regular ID for both Cal-ID and DL
    • Real ID is needed to get into federal buildings and to get on domestic flight starting on Oct 1, 2020
  4. NOTE: Name on ID generally must match SSA’s name, so change name with SSA first, if you are changing your name.
  5. NOTE: If applying for Driver’s license, there are additional requirements (they are outlined on DMV website).
  6. NOTE: If a person cannot prove their legal presence, they can apply for an AB 60 driver’s license, which allows a person to drive but is not a legal ID
    • They can consider also getting municipal ID (only some cities provide these) / consular ID card that might be helpful in some circumstances
  • Check your fees online
  • If senior, can get free senior ID
  • If homeless, in transitional housing, or in permanent housing but with history of inconsistent stability with housing and still at risk of losing housing, can get form signed by homeless service provider (for example, transitional home, agency that helps people who are homeless like Catholic Charities, parole/probation) to get no fee ID (Note that people must bring signed form to DMV)
  • If on public benefits, can get reduced fee ID
  1. If due to unpaid fees/fines ONLY (e.g., not also failure to appear), then you should no longer be suspended
  2. Get driver’s record to find out if suspension or revocation and reason for it.
    • Record can be retrieved for $2 online at Driver's Record Request
    • Record can also be retrieved for $5 by mail or by calling your local DMV
  3. Having trouble interpreting your online DMV record?
    • If the record is unclear, a DMV representative can explain what can be done (if not permanently revoked) to reinstate license by calling 1-800-777-0133
    • If you have failure to appear that is more than 5 years old, call the mandatory actions unit at (916) 657-6525 to see if DMV will remove suspension (See VC 12808).
    • If the DMV will not remove your suspension, you will need to appear in court (ask to be put on court calendar and have case move forward) to get your license reinstated.
