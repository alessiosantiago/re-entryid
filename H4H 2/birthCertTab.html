Obtaining Your Birth Certificate

A birth certificate can be obtained in person in county born (usually should check each county) or by mail or on the Internet - must prove ID (should lookup for address of county recorder that providers birth certificates and confirm that birth certificates are for all years at that county recorder)

If a close family member can get birth certificate for person, then birth certificate may be easiest to get, because family member can use their own photo ID to get it on personâ€™s behalf.In CA, eligible family members include: parent, legal guardian, child,grandparent, grandchild,sibling, spouse, domestic partner (and people ordered by a court)
If you must get documents by themselves, then you will need to find a notary who will verify their identity with the ID they have on hand, such as CDCR (prison or parole ID) ID, public benefits cards, or witnesses confirming their identity.

Tip: If a person does not have local contacts to confirm identity, the person can ask parole or probation officer (and their supervisor or other colleague, if they need a second person) to confirm their identity

Each county in California has its own form to use in person (going in person prevents the need for a notary and associated fee) and each state has its own form.

Mail-in CA form is here: CDPH Form
i. When a form is mailed in, it requires a notary to confirm identity, which could be an additional fee (some banks will offer notary services free if a person has a bank account)

ii. Fee waivers are available if a person is homeless with this form: CCR form. The form must be signed by a homeless service provider (this includes transitional housing providers). Catholic Charities, parole, and probation may be able to provide signed fee waivers.
